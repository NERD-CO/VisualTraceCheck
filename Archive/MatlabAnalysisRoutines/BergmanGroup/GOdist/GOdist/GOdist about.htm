About  GOdist:

 

GOdist is a MATLAB® program for analysis of distributions of transcript values associated with specific

gene ontology (GO) categories.

The continuous approach reveals subtle changes that occurred in many genes

linked to a category.

The discrete approach reveals significant changes that occurred in few genes in a category.

GOdist implements the “continuous” approach for analysis GO term expression

 patterns by employing Kolmogorov-Smirnov (KS) test.

     The discrete approach is implemented the hypergeometric distribution and a user defined threshold.

GOdist was designed to work with data from Affymterix®  microarrays and Gene Ontology Consortium (GOC) ontologies.

GOdist analysis was developed by Yoram Ben Shaul, an incumbent of a post-doctoral fellowship from the

Interdisciplinary Center for Computational Neuroscience instructed by Prof. Hagai Berman and Prof. Hermona Soreq.
